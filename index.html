Our Mission

The Institute of Personal Financial Coaching is dedicated to developing knowledgeable, ethical, and practical financial coaches who help individuals and families make informed decisions about money, debt, saving, investing, retirement, and long-term financial security.

Why Choose IPFC?

650 Education Hours

PFC candidates complete a substantial education pathway designed to build broad knowledge across major areas of personal finance.

16 Required Courses

The PFC pathway includes specified coursework in budgeting, investing, retirement, tax, insurance, credit, debt, and financial coaching.

100 Client Hours

Candidates document practical client-facing financial planning or coaching experience before final certification approval.

Master’s in Finance

All PFC candidates must have earned a master’s degree in finance from a regionally accredited university.

Capstone Event

Qualified candidates complete a proctored Capstone event using a family financial scenario to demonstrate practical planning skill.

Professional Standards

Education

IPFC promotes strong foundational knowledge in personal finance, budgeting, investing, retirement planning, insurance, taxation, estate planning, and financial behavior.

Ethics

Certified professionals are expected to act with honesty, integrity, transparency, and respect for the individuals and families they serve.

Verification

IPFC maintains a certification verification system so individuals may confirm credential status and certification information.

Featured Credential

PFC™

Personal Financial Coach™

The PFC™ designation recognizes individuals who have demonstrated competency in personal financial planning, coaching, retirement planning, investing fundamentals, debt management, risk management, taxation basics, and ethical financial education.

View PFC Requirements

Certification Pathway

1. Complete Education

Applicants complete approved personal finance, investing, retirement, tax, insurance, and estate planning coursework.

2. Submit Proof

Applicants provide documentation of completed courses, certificates, client hours, or approved professional learning.

3. Complete Capstone Event & Earn Certification

Applicants complete the required Capstone event. Upon successful completion, applicants receive a unique certification number and are added to the IPFC verification registry.
